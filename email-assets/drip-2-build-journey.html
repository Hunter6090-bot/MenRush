Hey,

Three days ago you joined the MenRush waitlist. I figured I owe you something more than a "thanks, you're on the list."

So here's where we actually are.

What's built:

– Live proximity backend. Postgres + PostGIS, custom-tuned to handle 10km-radius queries in under 50ms. (Translation: when you open the app, you'll see who's nearby instantly.)

– Auth, profile, photos, likes, matches, messaging — all working in dev.

– Real-time updates via WebSockets. No more "is he still online?" guessing.

What I've broken:

– Spent two days fighting Vercel's edge runtime before realising I needed to host the API on Railway.

– Tried to use Google Maps. Switched to Mapbox after looking at the price.

– Built a feature called "Heat Map" showing density of active men in a city. Killed it after three days because it felt creepy. Some features are too good — that doesn't mean they're right.

The part I'm proudest of: when you tap a profile, the distance updates in real-time as either of you moves. No refresh. Just live.

Next email in three days — I'll show you the map view.
